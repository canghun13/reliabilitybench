About the workbench

Built for the engineering conversation behind the metric.

ReliabilityBench is an independent practical-tool site for people who make availability, maintenance, asset, and MRO decisions in industrial operations.

What we are building

The site connects four workstreams that often get managed separately: reliability engineering, maintenance performance, asset and downtime economics, and MRO spare-parts planning. The goal is not to replace a CMMS, EAM, reliability study, or financial model. It is to make a calculation transparent and usable at the moment a team needs to frame a question.

Every calculator is designed to show what it does, the inputs it expects, the formula or method used, a worked example, interpretation, and known limits. That context is intentional: a bare number can look precise even when the data scope is wrong.

Our editorial standard

We prefer a smaller set of distinct, maintained tools to a large collection of near-duplicate calculators. Formulas are presented with their assumptions; units and boundaries are explicit; and practical notes point out conditions where a simple model should not decide the issue alone.

ReliabilityBench content is educational information, not site-specific engineering, safety, legal, financial, procurement, or compliance advice. Users remain responsible for validating inputs, governing standards, risk controls, and decisions in their own operating environment.

How to help improve it

If a formula needs clarification, a unit is unclear, or a tool lacks a field condition that matters in your work, please contact ReliabilityBench. Specific examples of the decision context are especially useful; do not send confidential equipment, production, or personal data.
